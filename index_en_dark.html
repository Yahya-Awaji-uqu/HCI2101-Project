Course Specification

Fundamentals of Human-Computer Interaction

Equipping students with HCI principles, UX fundamentals, and user-centered design thinking.

HCI 1201 4 Credit Hours Software Engineering
1

Course Description

This course provides students with a firm foundation in the main HCI principles and a working knowledge of HCI-oriented software development. Additionally, to familiarize students with the basic concepts of User Experience (UX) from understanding the UX to mapping user journey and highlighting touchpoints, pain points and opportunities.

2

Course Objectives

Equip students with a comprehensive understanding of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) principles, including essential design processes, user-centered research methodologies, and the psychological and cognitive theories that inform effective interface design. Emphasis will be placed on the integration of User Experience (UX) fundamentals.

3

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

# Domain Learning Outcome
1 Knowledge (K1) Describe foundational theories and models in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), including their historical development and relevance to user-centered technology design.
2 Skills (S2) Explain the role of cognitive and psychological theories in shaping user interaction and experience with technology and apply these insights to design solutions.
3 Skills (S4) Employ user-centered research methodologies, including user personas, task analysis, and usability testing, to inform the iterative design process.
4 Values (V2) Work in teams to review and analyze simple interactive systems that demonstrate an understanding of HCI principles and UX foundations.
5 Values (V5) Demonstrate creativity and empathy in developing HCI solutions, addressing real-world challenges by designing inclusive and socially responsible user experiences.
